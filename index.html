About Sensory Issues

What is Sensory Processing Disorder (SPD)?

According to WebMD, Sensory Processing disorder is where the brain has a difficult time recognizing and responding to sensory input from the body. Some people are oversensitive while others can me under sensitive. For those that are oversensitive sounds that for most of us are perfectly normal and not painful may be too loud. A shirt could chafe the skin resulting in the individual preferring only certain materials in their clothes. There is also a sensitivity to food that we may see. Some will not eat certain foods due more to consistency than to taste. Other symptoms of sensory processing disorder are being uncoordinated; bumping into things; inability to tell where their arms and legs are in space relative to where their body is; and it is difficult to engage in conversation or play.

Where is SPD seen?

While SPD is not a stand-alone diagnosis, meaning it occurs alongside other diagnosis like autism spectrum disorder, it is not only ASD, while common, that we see SPD in. ADD/ADHD is another one that has SPD in some of the patients. There are more but these are the two most common.

Sensory Tools to buy

Fidgets allow an individual to be productively distracted and occupy attention. This increases the persons focus and productivity by giving the mind a break which makes it easier to pay attention. Plus they are fun! Chewing Fidgets allow people with ADHD to focus better. They strengthen the jaw muscles and are a great way to relese pent up energy by chewing to stay on task. Plus they save clothes and pencils. The bouncing of a trampoline can burn off energy quickly but can also stimulate to allow a chance for concentration on the movement up and down. Plus they are fun!
The sensory swing can help to calm a person, improve attention and the ability to follow directions. They encourage a childs development as well sensory input and processing. Weighted blankets provide deep pressure stimulation which is thought to stimulate serotonin and reduce stress as well as increas melatonin levels that help you sleep. They may improve overall sleep quality. These improve the processing of sensory information. They help with attention focus, and learning.
